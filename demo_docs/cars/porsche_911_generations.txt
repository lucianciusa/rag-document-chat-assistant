Porsche 911 Water-Cooled Generations Overview

996 Generation (1999–2004)
- Notable aspect: First water-cooled 911. Controversial "fried egg" headlight design. 
- Major reliability issue: The IMS (Intermediate Shaft) bearing failure. Buyers must verify if the IMS bearing has been upgraded using an aftermarket kit, otherwise the engine runs a severe risk of catastrophic failure.

997 Generation (2005–2012)
- Return to round headlights. Considered a modern classic.
- The early years (997.1) still suffered from IMS bearing issues. The 997.2 (2009+) introduced the new 9A1 engine, completely eliminating the IMS bearing and introducing the ultra-fast PDK transmission.

991 Generation (2012–2019)
- Larger, more refined, and more GT-focused. 
- The 991.2 generation introduced twin-turbochargers for the standard Carrera models, replacing the glorious naturally aspirated flat-sixes previously used (which are now reserved only for GT3/GT3 RS models).

992 Generation (2020–Present)
- Current era. Features wide-body design across all models, massive interior tech upgrades, and a seamless lightbar across the rear. Extremely rapid performance but less analog feel.
